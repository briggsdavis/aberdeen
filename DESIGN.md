# Aberdeen Design Direction

Aberdeen should feel like a bright coastal restaurant with an editorial point of view: polished, playful, sunlit, and specific. The site should not read like a generic restaurant template. It should feel closer to a hospitality magazine cover, a printed seafood menu, or a table card from a room with real atmosphere.

## Core Vibe

- Coastal, editorial, organic, bright, and playful.
- More refined than casual seafood, less formal than a hotel dining room.
- Brand-led, image-led, and reservation-forward.
- Spacious layouts, strong color fields, big type, and confident asymmetry.
- Practical restaurant information should stay easy to find.

## Palette

- Aberdeen Blue: `#2A3B92`
- Aberdeen Peach: `#F9E1CA`
- Oyster White: `#FFF8F0`
- Kelp Ink: `#1D2A2F`
- Citrus: `#F7B733`
- Shell Pink: `#EFA6A0`

Blue should carry the structure and identity. Peach should add warmth and light. Avoid letting the site become a generic cream restaurant page.

## Typography

- Display: ED Venta Nueva Sans
- Body: Montserrat
- Utility: Montserrat
- Playful accent: Yaty

The type should be part of the identity, not neutral plumbing. Use display type with restraint and scale. Utility text should be compact, uppercase, and deliberate for nav, labels, hours, and short calls to action.

## Layout

The home page should move like an editorial restaurant story:

1. Hero with atmosphere, navigation, and a reservation path.
2. Short brand statement.
3. Menu doorways for Food, Spirits, and Beverages.
4. Image grid for ambiance and texture.
5. Reservation block.
6. Events teaser.
7. Location, hours, and footer.

Use full-width sections and strong bands of color. Avoid nested cards, decorative blobs, and generic numbered sections unless the content is truly sequential.

## Imagery

Imagery should be bright, coastal, tactile, and social:

- oysters and seafood details
- natural light on tables
- cocktails and bar moments
- room atmosphere
- dining textures, glassware, plates, linen, water, shell, citrus

Use real client photography when available. Unsplash imagery is temporary mood scaffolding.

## Logo Usage

- Peach wordmark: blue hero/nav contexts.
- Blue wordmark: light footer or peach/oyster backgrounds.
- Circle A: favicon and small brand stamp moments only.
- Standalone A: reserved for compact marks if needed.

Do not overuse the mark. The logo should feel placed, not sprinkled.

## Motion

Motion should be restrained:

- subtle image hover scale
- possible scroll reveals
- calm transitions for buttons and cards

One orchestrated moment is better than scattered animation. Respect reduced motion.

## Interaction Tone

Copy and controls should be direct:

- Reserve
- View food menu
- View events
- Plan a visit

No vague marketing filler. The site should help diners quickly understand the room, the food, and how to book.
